# LEAI Pitch — Spoken Script

Read this aloud. Square brackets are stage cues, not words to say.

Pacing target: about 120 words per minute. Total: ~600 words, ~5 minutes.

---

## Slide 1 — What is LEAI

[click]

Hi everyone. I want to share a tool from our lab called LEAI. [pause] LEAI stands for Learning Experience AI. [pause]

Think of it as a sibling tool to StudyHelper. StudyHelper helps students learn. LEAI listens to how it went. [pause]

It has three parts. The instructor uses Prompt Designer to set up a survey. The student opens Feedback Collector and has a short chat with an AI. The instructor then opens Feedback Analyzer to read the results. [pause]

I will walk through each part. The whole thing takes five minutes.

---

## Slide 2 — Instructor sets up a survey

[click]

This is the instructor side. [pause] You log in with a course ID. You pick a template. You write the bot's voice in plain words. You share a link with your students. [pause]

That is the full setup. It takes a few minutes, not hours. [pause]

You can run many surveys in one course. One per week is common. [pause]

There are three modes for the chat. Free-Form Chat. In-Group Feedback for team surveys. And Structured Reflection. [pause] I will explain Structured Reflection in a moment. First, let me show what the default chat looks like for the student.

---

## Slide 3 — Student conversation (default mode)

[click]

This is what the student sees. They open the link on a phone or a laptop. [pause] No login. No app. No account. [pause]

They give consent. Then the AI starts a short conversation. About five to ten minutes. [pause]

Four things matter here. The chat is conversational, so the AI asks follow-up questions. The student can stay anonymous. Or they can use a private code, if the instructor allows it. [pause] If the survey is a team survey, the student picks their team first. And students can speak instead of type, using the microphone. [pause]

This is the default mode. Open. Free-form. Good for general feedback.

---

## Slide 4 — Structured Reflection (form mode)

[click]

Now the other mode. Structured Reflection. [pause]

Here is what it does. The AI walks every section in order. The student cannot finish the chat until every section is covered. [pause]

Why does this exist? Some reflections need every section answered. For example, a graded team reflection. Each part of the rubric must be answered. [pause]

How does the instructor turn it on? In Prompt Designer, they pick a schema from a list. The schema is the list of sections. [pause] The student does not see the schema. They just see a normal chat. The structure is in the engine, not on the screen. [pause]

So you have two shapes. Free chat for open feedback. Structured Reflection when section coverage matters.

---

## Slide 5 — Instructor sees insights

[click]

Last part. The instructor opens Feedback Analyzer. [pause]

Free-form student chats are rich, but they are messy. Forty long conversations are hard to read. [pause]

So we designed the analyzer with three views. [pause]

The first is Quicktake (KWICK-take). It is an AI summary of the whole class. Fast to scan. [pause] The second is Keyness (KEE-ness). It uses statistics, not AI, to show which words are distinctive in your responses. [pause] The third is the raw student responses, one click away. [pause]

The reason for three views is trust. We did not want the instructor to rely on one black-box summary. With three views, you can triangulate. [pause]

That is LEAI. The instructor guide is in the repo if you want to look. Thank you.
