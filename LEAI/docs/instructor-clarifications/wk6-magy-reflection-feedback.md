## Message 2 — coverage / structure (the big one)

On hitting all the points: right now LEAI is more conversational — it touches the major aspects but improvises rather than marching through each section. Reading the template, you have ~10 distinct prompts (1.1 Key Concepts, 1.2 Methods, 1.3 Knowledge Shift with three sub-parts, 1.4 Capstone Connection, 2.1 Planning, 2.2 Roles, 2.3 Collaboration, 2.4 Team Health 1–5 ratings, 3.1 Open Question, 3.2 Commitment). Two ways I could enforce coverage — which do you prefer? (A) Strict agenda: the AI explicitly transitions through each section ("Now let's move to Methods in Practice…") and won't end the session until every prompt has at least one student response. (B) Soft coverage: the AI keeps a checklist internally, weaves the prompts naturally into the conversation, and only nudges toward missed sections near the end. More organic, but a student could give shallow answers on some sections. My instinct is (A) for the graded version since you need rubric-level coverage — but want to confirm.

## Message 5 — download format (revision + structure)

One thing worth flagging about the format shift: in the original PDF form, students can revise their answers freely before submitting. In LEAI they can't go back and edit — once they say something, it's in the transcript. If they want to revise, they'd have to express it in the conversation ("actually, scratch that, what I meant was…") and the AI would capture the updated version. So a question on the download you need for grading: (A) Raw transcript — the conversation as-is, in order. Preserves the natural flow and any in-conversation revisions, but you'd be reading back-and-forth dialogue rather than clean answers. (B) Structured form-mapped output — after the session, the AI consolidates each section's content into a clean answer under the template headers (1.1, 1.2, …), using the student's latest revision when they reworded something. Closer to what they'd hand in on the PDF, much easier to grade against the rubric — but it's the AI's summary of the student's words, not their verbatim writing. (C) Both — structured form on top for grading, raw transcript appended for verification. My lean is (C), but it depends on whether you want to grade what they *said* or what the AI *organized*. Let me know which feels right. Also — PDF or plain text/Markdown for the download? Course portal usually wants PDF per the submission guidelines.
