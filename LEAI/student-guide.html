How to complete your reflection in LEAI and submit it on Canvas — for both individual and group assignments.

What is LEAI?

LEAI is a chat-based reflection tool. Instead of filling in a static form, you have a short conversation with a reflection coach (the chatbot). It walks you through your reflection one area at a time, follows up if anything is unclear, and builds a Word document (.docx) of your answers that you download and upload to Canvas as your submission.

No account

You do not need to log in. Just open the link your instructor gave you on any device — phone, tablet, or laptop.

The screen, at a glance

Once the chat starts, every reflection has the same layout. Knowing the pieces up front makes the steps below easy to follow.

A live reflection. The labelled parts below appear on every screen.

Top bar

Shows LEAI, the reflection's title (e.g. Week 7 Individual Reflection), and — in individual mode — a green 🔒 ANONYMOUS badge so you know your responses aren't tied to your name. On the right are Terms and Privacy links and, once you start, a Copy resume link button (see Tips).

Progress strip

Just under the top bar, a line like "Area 1 of 4 — Key Concepts & Takeaways" tells you which part of the reflection you're on and how many areas there are in total.

Course banner

Your instructor may show a coloured notice strip (for example, a due-date reminder). If it has an ✕, you can close it; otherwise it stays put. It does not affect your submission.

Revision tip + input box

Above the text box, a yellow tip reminds you that you can revise or add to any answer at any time — just tell LEAI in plain words. Type your answer in the box, or click the Dictate (microphone) button to speak it, then click Send.

Save draft (.docx)

The strip at the bottom-right ("Reflection in progress · Save draft (.docx)") is your download button. It is available the whole time, and the file always reflects the latest state of your conversation. This is the file you upload to Canvas.

Which mode am I doing?

Your instructor will give you one of two links. They look similar but behave differently.

Individual

Anonymous individual reflection

Your responses are anonymous. There is a consent screen up front, then you chat through your reflection sections. Used for personal weekly reflections, mid-course feedback, etc.

Group

Identified team reflection

This is a graded reflection. Your responses — and the teammate names you mention — are visible to your instructor. You'll be asked to pick which team you are on before the chat starts.

Individual Reflection

Use this flow if your instructor gave you the individual link. The whole thing usually takes 5–10 minutes.

1
Open the link your instructor shared

It will look like https://guii-lab.github.io/LEAI/feedback.html?id=…. You can use any browser on any device.

2
Read the consent screen and click Continue

On the Before you begin screen, check the required box (the second optional research box is up to you), then click Continue. The button stays grey until the required box is checked.

The "Before you begin" modal. The first checkbox is required; the second is optional.
3
Chat through the areas

The chatbot walks you through the reflection one area at a time. Type your answer in the box at the bottom — or click the Dictate (microphone) button to speak it instead — and click Send. The bot often asks a quick follow-up before moving on. The progress strip near the top (e.g. "Area 1 of 4 — Key Concepts & Takeaways") shows how far along you are.

The chatbot opens with the first reflection area. Answer in the text box, or use the Dictate button.
4
Save your reflection (.docx) and upload to Canvas

Use the Save draft (.docx) button in the strip at the bottom-right to download your reflection as a Word file. It's available the whole time, so you don't have to wait — but once you've covered every area, the bot tells you the reflection is complete, so click it again then to grab the final version. Save the file, then upload that .docx to your Canvas assignment to submit.

The "Reflection in progress · Save draft (.docx)" strip is always under the input. Click it to download your .docx — the file reflects your latest answers.
Revise

You can revise or add to any answer at any time — just tell LEAI in plain words, like "go back to my answer about the deadline" or "add to what I said earlier". Everything still flows into the file. Click Save draft (.docx) again before uploading to grab the latest version.

Group Reflection

Use this flow if your instructor gave you the group/team link. This is a graded reflection — your name and your teammates' names will be visible to your instructor.

1
Open the team reflection link

It will look like https://guii-lab.github.io/LEAI/feedback.html?id=…. The page will open with a green-bordered "Before you start" card.

2
Read the graded-reflection notice

The first card explains that this is a graded reflection and that the names you mention will be visible to your instructor. Click I understand, continue →.

Group mode is identified, not anonymous. Use teammates' real names when the bot asks.
3
Pick your team

On the next screen, open the Pick your team dropdown and select your team number, then click Continue. If your team isn't listed, click "I don't see my team" and let your instructor know.

Pick your team from the dropdown.
Once you select a team, the Continue button turns green.
4
Chat through the areas

The chatbot walks you through the reflection one area at a time. Type your answer in the box at the bottom — or click the Dictate (microphone) button to speak it — and click Send. When the bot asks for teammate names, use their real names — the structured download follows your course's reflection-template format and the names go into the file. Note the top bar shows your team (e.g. "Team 2 · Graded reflection") with no anonymous badge, because this mode is identified.

In group mode the top bar names your team and reads "Graded reflection" — there is no ANONYMOUS badge.
5
Save your reflection (.docx) and upload to Canvas

Use the Save draft (.docx) button in the bottom-right strip to download your reflection as a Word file — the same control as in individual mode. When you've covered every area and the bot says the reflection is complete, click it to grab the final version, then upload that .docx to your Canvas assignment to submit.

Heads up

In group mode your responses are not anonymous. Your instructor will see your team number, your name, and the teammate names you mention. Keep your feedback constructive.

Tips & Tricks

Speak instead of typing

The Dictate button (the microphone next to Send) uses your browser's voice recognition to type straight into the input box. Works best in Chrome and Edge. Click it once to start, click again to stop, then review the text before you Send.

Finish later with the resume link

Click Copy resume link in the top bar to copy a link that reopens this exact reflection with your conversation intact. Email it to yourself (or paste it somewhere safe) if you want to take a break, switch devices, or come back tomorrow — then open that link to pick up where you left off.

You can take your time

There is no time limit and no autosave gap. You can leave the tab open while you think and come back to it — and with the resume link above, you don't lose your conversation even if you close the tab.

Open the file before submitting

Open the .docx in Word, Google Docs, or Pages once before uploading to Canvas. That way you confirm it actually contains your answers.

Common Questions

The Continue button is greyed out — what do I do?
Check the required consent box on the "Before you begin" screen. The button stays grey until that box is checked.
My team isn't in the dropdown.
Click "I don't see my team" on the team-selection screen and let your instructor know. Don't pick a wrong team — your reflection would be filed under the wrong group.
Can I change my answers after I'm done?
Yes. At any point — including after the bot says the reflection is complete — you can keep chatting to revise or add to any area; just tell LEAI in plain words. Click Save draft (.docx) again before uploading to Canvas — the file always reflects the most recent state of the conversation.
Can I finish later or switch to another device?
Yes. Click Copy resume link in the top bar and save that link (e.g. email it to yourself). Opening it reloads your reflection with the conversation intact, so you can pick up where you left off on any device.
It says "This survey has ended."
The submission window your instructor set has passed, so the reflection is closed and you can't submit through it. Contact your instructor on Canvas — they decide whether to reopen it.
The Dictate button isn't doing anything.
Your browser may be blocking microphone access, or it may not support voice input (try Chrome or Edge). You can always type your answers instead — the experience is the same.
Does my instructor see my private notes?
Only what you send to the chatbot is recorded. In individual mode, your responses are anonymous — your instructor sees the answers, not your name. In group mode, your responses are tied to your team and the names you mention are visible.
Where do I actually submit?
In Canvas, on the assignment your instructor created. The file you upload is the .docx you saved with the Save draft (.docx) button.

Questions or stuck? Email your instructor — they can see whether your submission came through.
