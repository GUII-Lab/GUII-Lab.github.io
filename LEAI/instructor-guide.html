Quick Start

If you want to run a feedback session today, follow these five steps. The rest of this guide covers each step in detail.

1
Log in to the Prompt Designer

Open the Prompt Designer page and enter your course ID and password. This is where you will create and manage all your surveys.

Open Prompt Designer →
Enter your course ID and dashboard password, then click Sign In.
2
Create a survey

Click one of the template buttons — Weekly Check-in is a good starting point. Give it a label (e.g. "Week 3 Check-in") and click Create Survey & Get Link.

Open Prompt Designer →
Clicking a template fills in the survey name and prompt automatically. You can edit any field before creating.
3
Copy the link and share it

Your new survey appears in the list on the right. Click Copy next to its link, then paste it into Canvas, a class slide, or an email. Students do not need an account — they just open the link.

Open Prompt Designer →
Each survey card shows its shareable link and a Copy button. The colored badge shows its current status.
4
Students complete the survey

Students open the link on any device — phone, tablet, or laptop. They have a short conversation with the AI (typically 5–10 minutes). No login, no app, no account required. When the class period ends, go back to the Prompt Designer and click Close on the survey to stop new responses.

What students see when they open the link. The AI greets them and asks a question based on your prompt.
5
Review results in the Feedback Analyzer

Open the Feedback Analyzer page and log in with the same course ID and password. Click View next to your survey to read student conversations. Use the CSV button to download a spreadsheet of all responses.

Open Feedback Analyzer →
The Feedback Analyzer shows total responses, average conversation depth, and a per-survey breakdown. Click CSV to download.

What is LEAI?

LEAI (Learning Experience AI) is a set of tools that lets you collect richer feedback from your students using AI-powered conversations. Instead of a static form, students have a short dialogue with an AI — it gently asks follow-up questions and encourages reflection. You then review the results in a dashboard.

Note

No AI expertise needed. You don't need to know how AI works to use these tools. Think of it like setting up a Google Form, except the form can ask follow-up questions on your behalf.

The Two Tools

LEAI has two pages you will use as an instructor:

Prompt Designer

Where you create and manage feedback surveys. Set the topic, choose settings, and share a link with students.

Feedback Analyzer

Where you see student responses. View conversation summaries, participation counts, and download raw data.

Logging In

Both tools use the same course ID and password that was set up for your course. You will need these two pieces of information:

  • Course ID — a short code like cm115-sp26
  • Dashboard password — chosen when your course was created
Tip

Keep your password safe. Anyone with your course ID and password can see student responses. Don't share it publicly.

Creating a Survey (Prompt Designer)

Open the Prompt Designer page. After logging in you will see the main dashboard. The left panel is where you build new surveys; the right panel shows all existing surveys for your course.

Using Templates

If you're not sure what to write, start with a template. Four pre-built templates cover the most common use cases.

Find the template buttons

Near the top of the left panel, you'll see four blue buttons: Weekly Check-in, Midterm Reflection, Project Debrief, and Exit Ticket.

Click a template

Click any template button. The survey name and instructions fields will fill in automatically with a ready-to-use prompt you can edit or use as-is.

Customize if needed

Read the pre-filled text. Change anything that doesn't fit your course. For example, swap "the project" for the actual project name.

After clicking "Weekly Check-in", the survey name and bot fields fill in automatically. The hints beneath Bot Name and Bot Role explain what each field does — students see the bot name in their chat window.
The Bot Persona section on an empty form. "Bot Name" is what students see in chat. "Bot Role" shapes the AI's tone — for example, "friendly teaching assistant" produces warmer, more conversational responses than "feedback bot".

Survey Settings

Below the main prompt area is a Survey Settings panel. You can expand it by clicking the heading. These settings control how and when students can participate.

Anonymity

All LEAI surveys are anonymous. No name, email, or student ID is collected — sessions appear as "Anonymous Student 1, 2…" in the Feedback Analyzer.

Opens At / Expires At

Set a date range for when the survey is active. Students who visit before the open date, or after the expiry date, will see a polite message instead of the chat. Leave blank to use the default expiry of 14 days from creation.

The Survey Settings panel. Click the heading to expand it. All fields are optional — you can create a survey with just a name and prompt.
Tip

Not sure about settings? Leave them at their defaults. You can always edit them later using the Edit button on the survey card.

Reading Status Badges

Each survey card in the right panel shows a colored badge telling you its current status at a glance.

● Open ● Closed ● Expired ● Scheduled
  • Open — students can access and submit responses right now
  • Closed — you manually closed it; students see a "closed" message
  • Expired — the expiry date has passed; no new responses accepted
  • Scheduled — the open date is in the future; not yet visible to students
Survey cards in the Prompt Designer. Each card shows the status badge, expiry date, and action buttons.

Closing & Reopening a Survey

You can manually close a survey at any time — for example, when a class session ends. You can also reopen it later.

To close a survey

Find the survey card and click the Close button. The badge will immediately change to Closed. Students who visit the link will see a message saying the survey is closed.

Click the Close button on any Open survey to stop accepting responses immediately.
To reopen a survey

On a closed survey card, click the Reopen button. The survey becomes Open again, and the expiry date is automatically extended by 14 days from today.

After clicking Reopen, the badge changes back to Open and a new expiry date is set 14 days from now.

Editing a Survey

You can change a survey's label, week number, instructions, and date range after it has been created.

Click Edit

Click the Edit button on any survey card. A panel slides open with the current values already filled in.

Make your changes

Update any fields you want. You can change the label, instructions, week number, or date range.

Click Save

Click the Save Changes button. The survey card updates immediately to reflect the new values.

The Edit panel opens with the survey's current values. Change what you need, then click Save Changes.
After saving, the survey card updates instantly — no page refresh needed.

Duplicating a Survey

If you want to reuse a survey from a previous week, use the Duplicate button instead of retyping everything.

Click Duplicate

Click the Duplicate button on any survey card. A copy is created with the same prompt and settings, but a fresh link and Open status.

Edit the copy

The new survey appears in the list. Use the Edit button to update its label, week number, or instructions before sharing the new link with students.

After clicking Duplicate, a new survey card appears at the bottom of the list. The copy is immediately Open and ready to use.

Analyzing Feedback

The Feedback Analyzer is your central dashboard for understanding what students are saying. It offers three layers of analysis — each designed so you can go as shallow or as deep as you need:

  • Traditional Analysis — deterministic word-frequency bars you can verify yourself (no AI involved)
  • AI Quick Take — a one-click summary with citations so you can trace every claim back to a student response
  • Feedback Chat — a full chat interface where you ask free-form questions about your data and the AI answers with evidence

Open the Feedback Analyzer from the sidebar (or navigate directly) and log in with your course ID and dashboard password — the same credentials you use for the Prompt Designer.

The Dashboard

After logging in you see the full dashboard for your course. It has four sections stacked top to bottom:

Scope chips

A row of buttons at the top: Entire Course plus one chip per weekly survey. Click any chip to filter everything on the page to that scope.

Stats row

Four cards showing: total Responses, Participation percentage, Avg Words per response, and a Responses / Week sparkline so you can spot drop-offs at a glance.

Traditional Analysis

Horizontal bar chart of the most frequent terms in student feedback. Toggle between Unigram, Bigram, and Trigram. Click any bar to see the exact responses that contain that term.

AI Quick Take & Student Responses

Below the bars you'll find the AI-generated summary (if you've triggered one) and the full list of student responses in collapsible cards.

The Analyzer dashboard in Entire Course scope. Scope chips across the top, four stat cards, and the Traditional Analysis bar chart showing bigrams by frequency.

Filtering by Scope

The scope chips at the top of the dashboard control what data appears everywhere on the page — stats, n-gram bars, Quick Take, and response list all update instantly when you select a different scope.

  • Entire Course — shows every student response across all weeks. The Keyness toggle is disabled because there is no reference subset to compare against.
  • Week N — shows only responses from that week's survey. The Keyness toggle becomes active, letting you see which terms are statistically distinctive for that week compared to the full course.
Tip

Use scope to spot weekly shifts. Click through Week 1 → Week 2 → Week 3 and watch the n-gram bars change. Terms that appear only in later weeks often signal emerging issues worth addressing.

The dashboard filtered to Week 1 Check-in. Stats, n-gram bars, and all other sections update to show only that week's data. Note the Keyness toggle is now available in the bar chart controls.

Traditional Analysis (N-grams)

The Traditional Analysis panel shows you what students are talking about using pure word-frequency counting — no AI is involved, so the results are completely deterministic and reproducible.

Three term-length modes:

  • Unigram — single words (e.g. "game", "confusing", "help")
  • Bigram — two-word pairs (e.g. "group project", "office hours") — the default view
  • Trigram — three-word phrases (e.g. "too much group")

Two ranking modes:

  • Freq — raw count: how many times the term appears
  • Keyness — (only available when a weekly scope is selected) — ranks terms by how statistically over-represented they are in the selected week compared to the full course. Uses a log-likelihood formula. A high keyness score means the term is distinctively associated with that week's feedback.

Clicking a bar expands it to show every student response that contains that term, with the keyword highlighted in gold. Each match card shows a response ID (R1, R2, etc.) and the week label so you can cross-reference it with the full response list below.

Clicking a bar reveals matching responses. Here the game design bigram is expanded, showing the two student responses that contain it with keywords highlighted.
Switching to Keyness ranking (available when a weekly scope is selected) reorders terms by how statistically distinctive they are for that week compared to the full course.
Note

Common English words are filtered out. The n-gram analysis automatically removes stopwords like "the", "is", "and", "I", etc. so the bars show meaningful content words only.

AI Quick Take

The AI Quick Take gives you a concise, citation-backed summary of the feedback in the current scope. Unlike the traditional analysis, this does use AI — but every claim includes numbered citations so you can verify it against the original student responses.

Click "Generate summary"

The Quick Take is generated on demand — it won't run automatically. You'll see a shimmer animation while the AI processes the responses (typically 10–20 seconds).

Read the bullet points

The AI returns 8–15 themed bullet points covering recurring praise, concerns, and suggestions. Each bullet ends with numbered citation pills like [1] [2] [3].

Check the verification badge

The top-right shows something like 62/66 verified. This means a second AI pass checked each citation against the source text. Teal pills are verified; gold pills are flagged as partially supported.

Hover a pill to see the source

Hover over any numbered citation pill to see which response ID (R1, R2, etc.) it refers to. You can scroll down to the Student Responses section to find that response.

Important

Minimum 20 responses required. The Quick Take will not generate if the current scope has fewer than 20 student responses. This prevents the AI from making broad claims from too little data. Switch to "Entire Course" scope if a single week doesn't meet the threshold.

The Quick Take is cached on the server — once generated, it reloads instantly on future visits. Click Regenerate to create a fresh summary (for example, after new responses come in). Click Show prompt to see the exact instruction sent to the AI.

The AI Quick Take for the full course. Each bullet summarises a theme with inline citation pills (numbered, teal = verified). The Verified badge at top-right shows how many citations passed a second-pass check.

Student Responses

The Student Responses section appears at the bottom of the dashboard. Each student's feedback session is shown as a collapsible card. Anonymous surveys display "Anonymous Student 1", "Anonymous Student 2", and so on.

Collapsed by default

Each card shows the student label, the week, and a message count. This gives you a quick sense of participation before diving in.

Click to expand

Click any card to reveal that student's full messages. Each message is labelled STUDENT so the source is clear.

Expand All / Collapse All

Use the button at the top-right of the section to open every card at once for a full read-through.

Tip

Looking for a specific theme? Use your browser's Find (Cmd+F / Ctrl+F) while all cards are expanded to search across all student messages at once.

The Student Responses section with cards expanded. Each card shows the student label and their messages. Use Expand All to open every card at once.

Feedback Chat

For deeper exploration, open the Feedback Chat page from the sidebar. This is a full chat workspace where you can ask free-form questions about your survey data and the AI responds with evidence-backed answers.

A fresh Feedback Chat session. The sidebar lists saved sessions, and quick-prompt suggestions help you get started.
Click "+ New Chat"

This creates a new chat session scoped to your entire course. Quick-prompt suggestions appear to help you get started — click one or type your own question.

Ask a question

Type a question like "What are students struggling with?" or "Group the responses into clusters" and press Enter. The AI reads all student responses in scope and answers with inline numbered citations.

Read the cited response

Each AI answer includes a Verified X/Y badge. Numbered citation pills are embedded in the text — teal means the citation was verified against the source; gold means it's partially supported. Hover a pill to see the response ID.

Continue the conversation

Ask follow-up questions in the same session. The AI remembers the context from earlier messages, so you can drill deeper ("Tell me more about the grading complaints") without repeating yourself.

Sessions are persistent. Chat sessions are saved on the server and appear in the left sidebar. You can switch between sessions, rename them, export a session as Markdown, or delete sessions you no longer need. The session title is automatically generated from your first message.

A chat conversation in progress. The AI response uses bold headings, bullet lists, and numbered citation pills. Hover a pill to see which student response it refers to.
Tip

Use the "Ask a follow-up" link. After reviewing the AI Quick Take on the Analyzer dashboard, click Ask a follow-up → to jump directly into a new chat session pre-loaded with the Quick Take context.

Customising the AI Prompt

The Feedback Chat gives you full control over the AI's behaviour. Click the System Prompt button below the text input to open the prompt editor.

Open the prompt editor

Click the small System Prompt button beneath the message input. A popup appears with the current system prompt in an editable text field.

Edit the prompt

Change the instructions to focus the AI on a specific area — for example, add "Focus only on responses related to grading" or "Respond in bullet points only." Changes apply to future messages in this session.

Restore default

Click Restore default in the popup to reset to the standard LEAI prompt at any time.

Replay last turn

Click Replay last turn to re-send your last question with the updated prompt. This lets you quickly compare how different prompts change the AI's response.

The System Prompt popup. Edit the instructions, click Done to save, or use Restore default to reset. Replay last turn re-sends your last question with the updated prompt.
Note

Prompt changes are per-session. Each chat session stores its own system prompt override. Creating a new session always starts with the default prompt. This means you can experiment freely without affecting other sessions.

What Students See

When you share a survey link with students, they land on a simple chat page. Understanding what they see helps you explain the activity and troubleshoot issues.

The Chat Interface

The student sees a minimal chat window. The AI opens with a greeting question based on your prompt. Students type their response and press Send. The conversation continues naturally for a few turns.

The header of the chat page makes the anonymity guarantee visible at all times: a green ANONYMOUS badge sits next to the survey title, and the subtitle reads "Your responses are anonymous." Two small buttons — Terms and Privacy — in the top-right let students open the legal documents in a new tab at any point during the session.

The student chat page. Note the green ANONYMOUS badge next to the title, the "Your responses are anonymous" subtitle underneath, and the Terms / Privacy links at the top-right, which open the legal documents in a new tab at any time during the session.

Consent & Anonymity Notice

The first time a student opens a feedback link in a browser session, they are shown a small consent dialog before the chat begins. This is how LEAI records informed consent in a way that meets industry best practice for a research-adjacent tool — without being scary or bureaucratic.

The consent dialog. Students must check the required box (which contains inline links to the Terms of Use and Privacy Policy, opening in a new tab) to continue. The optional research consent box is separate.

What the dialog says, in plain terms:

  • LEAI is an anonymous mid-course feedback tool. No name, email, student ID, or login is collected — only a random session code that is discarded when the student closes the tab.
  • The purpose is to help improve the course while it's still running, not to wait for end-of-quarter evaluations when it is too late to help current students.
  • Responses are aggregated and cannot be traced back to individuals. Participation is voluntary and does not affect grades.
  • There are two checkboxes. The first (required) records agreement to the Terms of Use and Privacy Policy — the document names are inline links that open the full text in a new browser tab. The second (optional) records research consent for that session: it is persisted to the backend as a per-message flag, so any future GUII Lab research analysis can be restricted to messages where the student opted in.

The Terms of Use and the Privacy Policy live as their own standalone pages (LEAI/legal/terms.html and LEAI/legal/privacy.html). The canonical source of each document is the corresponding Word file (LEAI-Terms-of-Use.docx and LEAI-Privacy-Policy.docx) in the same folder — edit those when the policy needs to change. After editing, run LEAI/legal/scripts/regenerate-md-from-docx.sh to refresh the matching terms.md / privacy.md files that the live HTML pages render. The edits then appear on the hosted pages with no code change.

How often will students see this?

The consent dialog appears once per browser session. If a student refreshes the page or follows a new survey link in the same tab, the dialog is skipped. Closing the tab and returning later is treated as a fresh session, so the dialog reappears — and the research consent checkbox resets, by design, so the student can freshly choose each time.

What if a student wants to re-read the docs during the chat?

The Terms and Privacy buttons in the top-right of the header open the respective documents in a new browser tab at any point during the conversation. The full text of each document is a real page — students (and instructors) can bookmark it, share it, or even print it.

What If a Student Opens the Wrong Link?

If a student navigates to the feedback page without a valid survey link (for example, if they bookmark the base URL instead of the survey-specific link), they will see a clear message rather than a broken or blank page.

Students who open the feedback page without a survey link see this screen. They cannot browse other surveys — only the direct link shared by the instructor works.

What Happens When a Survey Is Closed or Expired

If a student tries to access a survey that is no longer active, they will see a clear message explaining why — they won't just get an error.

When a survey is manually closed, students see this screen with a lock icon and a clear explanation.
When a survey's expiry date has passed, students see this screen. The message gently explains the deadline.
Warning

Students got a "closed" screen but the survey should be open? Go to the Prompt Designer, find the survey, and check the Opens At and Expires At dates. If the expiry date is in the past, click Reopen to reset it.

Common Questions

Can students see each other's responses?
No. Each student's conversation is private. Only you can view responses through the Feedback Analyzer.
How long does a typical student session take?
Most students finish in 3–8 minutes. The AI doesn't require a minimum number of messages, but it will gently encourage a bit of depth if a student responds very briefly.
Can I reuse a survey from a previous quarter?
Yes. Use the Duplicate button to create a fresh copy with a new link. Then edit the label and dates before sharing.
A student says the link isn't working. What should I check?
Go to the Prompt Designer and find the survey. Check that the status badge is Open. If it says Closed or Expired, use the Reopen button. Also confirm the Opens At date is not in the future.
Do students need to create an account?
No. Students just open the link. No login, no account, no app to install.
Can I edit the survey after students have already started responding?
You can edit the label, dates, and week number at any time. Editing the prompt (the actual AI instructions) will only affect conversations that start after the edit — existing conversations are not changed.
What does "Avg. Turns = 1" mean?
It means most students sent one message and the AI replied once. This often indicates students answered very briefly. You might adjust your prompt to ask a more open-ended question, or tell students in class that you're looking for a few exchanges.
I ran the AI analysis, then refreshed the page and it's gone. What happened?
Analysis results are saved automatically in your browser when you run them. They will reload the next time you open that survey's detail view — as long as you're using the same browser and haven't cleared browser data. If the results are missing, just click Run Analysis again.
How do I read individual student responses?
Open the Survey Detail view (click View → on any survey row). Scroll to the Student Responses section at the bottom. Each card represents one student's session. Click a card to expand it and read the full conversation. Use Expand All to open every conversation at once.

Glossary

Term Plain-language definition
Survey An AI-powered feedback session you create and share with students
Prompt The instructions you write telling the AI what topic to discuss with students
Turn One student message plus one AI reply = one turn
Session One student's complete conversation with the AI on a particular survey
Response A student who submitted at least one message (counted once per survey)
AI Analysis An AI-generated summary of themes and friction points from student conversations, available in the Survey Detail view
CSV A spreadsheet file format that opens in Excel, Google Sheets, or Numbers
Course ID A short code identifying your course, used to log in to both LEAI tools
