# StudyCrafter Meeting — LEAI Walkthrough Script

> About 5 minutes. Read at roughly 120 words per minute.
> Pause briefly at every period. Stage cues are in square brackets:
> `[pause]` for breath, `[click]` to advance the slide.

---

## Slide 1 — What is LEAI

Hi everyone. [pause]
Today I want to share LEAI with you. [pause]
LEAI stands for Learning Experience AI. [pause]
It is a sibling tool to StudyHelper. [pause]
Same lab. Different focus. [pause]
The simple way to think about it. [pause]
StudyHelper helps students learn. [pause]
LEAI listens to how it went. [pause]
LEAI has three parts. [pause]
Prompt Designer is where instructors set up a survey. [pause]
Feedback Collector is where students give feedback. [pause]
Feedback Analyzer is where instructors read what students said. [pause]
I will show you all three.

[click]

---

## Slide 2 — Instructor sets up a survey

This is Prompt Designer. [pause]
An instructor opens it. [pause]
They pick a template. [pause]
They edit the prompt a little. [pause]
Then they copy a link. [pause]
That link is what they share with students. [pause]
The whole setup takes a few minutes. [pause]
One course can have many surveys. [pause]
For example, one survey per week. [pause]
Or one for each project milestone. [pause]
There are three modes here. [pause]
Free-form chat. In-Group feedback. And Structured Reflection. [pause]
The next two slides will show what students see.

[click]

---

## Slide 3 — Student conversation

This is Feedback Collector. [pause]
The student opens the link. [pause]
No login. No app. No account. [pause]
They have a short conversation with the AI. [pause]
Usually five to ten minutes. [pause]
By default it is anonymous. [pause]
The instructor can also choose pseudonymous mode. [pause]
That means students enter a short identifier — like a nickname. [pause]
For group projects, students can reflect as a team. [pause]
Voice input is supported, for students who do not want to type. [pause]
At the end, the student gets a completion code. [pause]
That gives the instructor proof, if they need it.

[click]

---

## Slide 4 — Structured Reflection

This is a different mode. [pause]
It is called Structured Reflection. [pause]
What is it? [pause]
The instructor picks a schema. [pause]
A schema is a fixed list of sections to cover. [pause]
You can see one on the screen. [pause]
This one has six sections. [pause]
The AI walks through every section in order. [pause]
Why does this exist? [pause]
Free chat is good for open feedback. [pause]
But sometimes every section has to be answered. [pause]
For example, a graded team reflection. [pause]
Structured Reflection makes sure no section is skipped. [pause]
How is it used? [pause]
The instructor turns it on in Prompt Designer. [pause]
They just pick a schema. [pause]
The student still sees a normal chat. [pause]
They do not need to know about the schema.

[click]

---

## Slide 5 — Instructor sees insights

This is Feedback Analyzer. [pause]
We built this because reading forty student chats by hand is slow. [pause]
Patterns hide in the transcripts. [pause]
Our design has one main idea. [pause]
Summary first. Evidence one click away. [pause]
At the top is Quick Take. [pause]
That is a short summary of what students said this week. [pause]
Below it are key words and phrases. [pause]
We call this keyness — KEY-ness. [pause]
It shows what stood out this week, compared to the whole course. [pause]
You can switch between single words, two-word phrases, and three-word phrases. [pause]
We call those n-grams. EN-grams. [pause]
And every word or phrase is clickable. [pause]
One click takes you to the actual student lines. [pause]
For Structured Reflection, the Analyzer also shows insights per section. [pause]
That is LEAI. [pause]
The instructor guide on our site has all the details. [pause]
Thank you.

---

## Pronunciation guide

- **LEAI** — say each letter: L-E-A-I.
- **keyness** — KEY-ness (rhymes with "highness").
- **n-grams** — EN-grams (the letter "N" then "grams").
- **schema** — SKEE-muh.
- **pseudonymous** — soo-DON-ih-mus (the "p" is silent).
